Letters from Soldiers

I made a vow to God. If I made it out of this war alive, I would carry my youngest daughter on my back to stairs of the cathedral to be baptized.

My wife keeps asking me, “When will you return?” When will I return?

“Soon,” I said, “once the infantry leaves the shores of Iwo Jima. I’ll be home before the end of the summer.” We’re preparing for the invasion of Japan.

“She walked for the first time today. A few bumbling steps, but she marches forward.”

How many days has it been since we landed on this hellhole? I don’t know. The days blur together.

These men are of the old breed, these men are our commanding officer. The very men put in charge of our lives. I can now see white sands of Pacific before me.
